# Community code of conduct

NeuMan contributors and maintainers are expected to make participation safe, professional, and constructive.

Be respectful; critique work rather than people; welcome questions asked in good faith; credit others' work; protect private information and unreleased game assets; and avoid harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized conduct, deliberate disruption, or publishing another person's credentials or identifying information.

Project maintainers may edit, reject, hide, or remove contributions and may temporarily or permanently restrict participation when conduct threatens people, the project, or its users. Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately, and avoid unnecessary disclosure.

Report conduct concerns through a private maintainer channel in the canonical GitHub repository. For security issues, use the process in `SECURITY.md`. Retaliation against a good-faith reporter is prohibited.

This policy applies in project spaces and when someone publicly represents the project. It may be refined through the governance process without reducing protections for participants.
